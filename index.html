how to use this (read this once, i promise it's worth it):
  • what even is cgpa? it's just a weighted average of all your grade points, weighted by credits. that's it. nothing magical.
  • grade point lookup: S = 10  ·  A+ = 9  ·  A = 8.5  ·  B+ = 8  ·  B = 7.5  ·  C+ = 7  ·  C = 6.5  ·  D = 6  ·  P = 5.5
  • credits: the number next to each subject in your syllabus/timetable. theory = 3 or 4, labs = 1 or 2. check RSMS or your scheme of study document.
  • sgpa: your semester GPA — one number for that whole semester. it's on your grade card / RSMS results page.
the recommended flow if you're mid-semester (e.g. S5 results just dropped):
  • step 1 → click "by semester". add each completed semester — name (S1, S2...), SGPA from grade card, total credits that sem. repeat for all done sems.
  • step 2 → click "by subject". your previous CGPA and total credits will auto-fill from what you entered in step 1. no need to retype anything.
  • step 3 → enter how many subjects you have this semester (count from your timetable). hit "let's go".
  • step 4 → add each subject one by one — name, grade point (use lookup above), and credits. enter as you get results, or enter expected ones to see projections.
  • step 5 → once all subjects are entered, your updated CGPA appears. the result is locked/greyed out until then so you don't misread a partial number.
  • first semester? skip step 1 entirely. go straight to "by subject", put 0 for previous CGPA and 0 for previous credits.
→ find your SGPA on your grade card or RSMS portal. one row per semester, that's it.

attendance — the one thing that can betray you even if you study:
  • minimum required: 75% at most colleges. some departments are extra and want 85%. check with your class coordinator (or pretend you didn't know).
  • safe misses: how many more classes you can bunk without crossing the line. use wisely. or don't. your call.
  • shortage: you'll see how many classes you need to attend in a row to recover. yes, consecutively. no skipping in between.
  • projection grid: shows what happens to your % as more classes happen and you miss more (or stop missing). useful for planning your bunkage strategy.
